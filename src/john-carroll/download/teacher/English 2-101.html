Grading
-Grades for individual assignments will receive a weight based on the level of time involved and/or their importance.
Single Weight: minor assignments and quizzes
Double Weight: summer reading test, book reports, essays
Triple Weight: unit tests, research paper

 Class Policies
-You are expected to attend class on time, have all of your necessary materials, and conduct yourself in a proper manner. Necessary materials include your textbooks, computer, pen, etc. Excessive lateness will not be tolerated.

-This course will be managed through Veracross, and you will be expected to check for homework assignments, quiz/test dates, and other important information. You will also be required to submit assignments to Veracross and/or JCS email.

-If you miss class for any reason, you are responsible for making up any work that you miss. The class/homework assignment can be obtained by checking Veracross. If you are out for a significant period of time, you can notify me through e-mail (mblair@johncarroll.org). Quizzes or tests must be made up within three days of your return to school.

-You will be penalized for any assignment that is turned in late. Please be aware that there is a new schoolwide policy on late work, which does not allow for work to be turned in late beyond a complete 8-day cycle.

-All students are expected to participate in class discussions and remain focused and “on-task” during class assignments. Class participation should always be respectful of others in the class and should only come when you have been given permission to speak. The more you participate in class discussions and activities, the more you will gain from this course.

-The John Carroll School has an Honor Pledge that is displayed in all classrooms. It is the expectation of the entire school community that all students will abide by this pledge. In order to ensure compliance with this pledge, familiarize yourself with the Student Handbook's definition of cheating and plagiarism. Anyone who violates the terms of these rules will be sanctioned accordingly.

-Visits to the restroom should take place between classes and during free mods; however, occasionally it becomes necessary to use the restrooms during class. This will be permitted so long as it is not abused. If this privilege is abused, visits will be limited.

-If you need any additional help, I'm available before and after school and during my free mods.
